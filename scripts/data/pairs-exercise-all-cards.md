Exercise cards that require exactly two people. The partner supplies timing, cueing, resistance, support, pressure, catch/throw exchange, or live movement stimulus. Drills requiring groups of three or more are intentionally excluded.

- **Partner Medicine Ball Throws, Catches, and Rotational Power** (01-10): Two-person medicine-ball drills that train throw timing, trunk rotation, catch readiness, relocation, deceleration, and clean force transfer without becoming a random conditioning game.
- **Paired Strength, Carries, and Manual Resistance** (11-20): Two-person resistance drills where the partner supplies controlled load, drag, assistance, or opposition so the athlete builds force, posture, grip, and tissue tolerance with clear role switching.
- **Partner Core, Balance, and Perturbation Control** (21-30): Two-person drills that use touch, taps, band pull, ball exchange, or partner pressure to train trunk control, balance, anti-rotation, and contact recovery.
- **Reactive Agility, Chase, Mirror, and Decision Drills** (31-40): Paired movement games where the second athlete supplies the live stimulus. The goal is perception-action quality, clean acceleration, braking, and reset behavior, not group chaos or fatigue.
- **Contact, Wrestling, Gymnastics Support, and Cooperative Locomotion** (41-50): Two-person contact and cooperative body-control drills for low stance, hand fighting, crawling, supported locomotion, assisted transitions, and shared force management.
